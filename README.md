# Netflix Content Analysis using Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

## Project Overview

This project presents a comprehensive Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) of the Netflix Movies and TV Shows dataset. The objective is to uncover meaningful insights, identify trends, analyze content distribution, and understand patterns within Netflix's content library using data visualization and statistical analysis techniques.

The analysis focuses on content types, release trends, country-wise distribution, content ratings, genres, movie durations, directors, actors, and overall business insights.

---

## Objectives

- Perform data exploration and statistical analysis.
- Identify missing values and data quality issues.
- Analyze Netflix content growth over time.
- Explore country-wise content distribution.
- Examine content ratings and audience targeting.
- Identify popular genres on Netflix.
- Analyze movie duration patterns.
- Discover top directors and actors featured on the platform.
- Generate business insights and recommendations based on the analysis.

## Key Findings

- Movies significantly outnumber TV Shows on Netflix.
- Netflix experienced rapid content growth after 2015.
- The United States contributes the largest share of Netflix content.
- International Movies, Dramas, and Comedies are among the most common genres.
- TV-MA and TV-14 are the most frequently occurring content ratings.
- Most movies have a duration between 80 and 120 minutes.
- Netflix maintains a globally diverse content portfolio.
- A limited number of directors and actors appear across multiple titles.

---

## Business Insights

- Netflix heavily focuses on modern content to meet current audience preferences.
- International content plays a significant role in platform growth.
- Popular genres such as Drama and Comedy drive audience engagement.
- Content diversification supports Netflix's global expansion strategy.
- Understanding audience preferences can improve future content acquisition decisions.

## Business Recommendations
